the jungle

Working closely with the management team for Glass Animals, we were tasked with bringing audiences back to the band’s debut album, Zaba, in a way that reignited the magic of its original release. Known for its blend of electronic, indie pop, and psychedelic influences, Zaba drew inspiration from the otherworldly landscapes of William Steig’s, The Zabajaba Jungle. Our goal was to create an experience that didn’t just revisit the music, but fully immersed fans in the dreamlike quality of the album.

immersion

Using a 360-degree video format, we transformed Zaba’s jungle-like atmosphere into a multi-sensory journey. Fans could wander through fluorescent forests, swim through kaleidoscopic landscapes, and discover hidden details embedded in the environment. Each scene encouraged exploration, offering subtle surprises that rewarded curiosity, making it feel as though they were moving deeper into the jungle.

alignment

Collaborating closely with artist managers ensured the project stayed true to the band’s original vision. The dynamic sound design, vibrant visuals, and layered textures, created a rich, multi-sensory experience that allowed fans to immersive themselves in the album that started it all.
